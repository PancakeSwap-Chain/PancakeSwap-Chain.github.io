What is PancakeSwap Chain?

PancakeSwap is a decentralized exchange and automated market maker for token swaps, liquidity, and related DeFi tools across supported networks. The term usually describes the PancakeSwap chain and network experience rather than a separate blockchain, and Investopedia's automated market maker overview is useful background for the DEX model before opening the app.

What chain is PancakeSwap on?

PancakeSwap started on BNB Chain and is still most closely associated with BNB Smart Chain trading, but it is no longer a single-chain DEX. The PancakeSwap product overview describes the current multichain product set, while DefiLlama's PancakeSwap AMM dashboard is a separate data view for the protocol. The exact route a user sees depends on the token pair, wallet network, and whether the app is using same-chain swap, cross-chain swap, or a specific product such as Infinity.

PancakeSwap BNB Chain fees and swap costs

A PancakeSwap trade on BNB Chain includes the network gas fee paid in BNB plus the DEX pool or route fee embedded in the swap quote. DefiLlama's fees and volume view helps separate protocol activity from a single user's execution quote, but the effective cost also includes price impact, slippage, and any routing through multiple pools. Before confirming, users should check minimum received, route details, gas token balance, and whether the wallet is on BNB Chain or another supported chain.

PancakeSwap supported chains for CAKE and swaps

PancakeSwap supports swaps beyond BNB Chain, including Ethereum, Solana, Base, Arbitrum, Aptos, ZKsync, Linea, Monad, and opBNB according to its current product documentation. CoinGecko's CAKE token page lists token ecosystem and explorer references, which is why the token contract address matters before a wallet signs. A CAKE balance on BNB Chain is not automatically the same as a CAKE balance on Ethereum or Aptos, so users should switch the wallet to the intended network and avoid lookalike CAKE tokens promoted in search ads.

PancakeSwap CAKE staking and chain choice

CAKE staking and PancakeSwap chain selection are related but not identical. Swapping can happen on several supported chains, while CAKE staking or reward products may be available only in specific app sections and networks. A user who wants to stake CAKE should compare the app's live Earn section with the PancakeSwap token listing and confirm the wallet network before approving tokens. Yield rates are not fixed forever; they can depend on emissions, fee sharing, pool rules, and governance changes.

PancakeSwap Chain FAQ

Is PancakeSwap only on BNB Chain?

No. The BNB Chain official site describes BNB Smart Chain as EVM-compatible, while the current PancakeSwap app supports several chains. BNB Chain remains central to PancakeSwap's identity and CAKE liquidity, and users should always confirm the selected chain in the wallet before swapping.

What gas token do I need for PancakeSwap?

The gas token depends on the selected chain. Investopedia's gas-fee explainer describes why transaction fees are paid in the native asset of the network: on BNB Chain you need BNB, on Ethereum you need ETH, on Base you need ETH on Base, and on Solana you need SOL. CAKE is not the universal gas token for PancakeSwap trades.

Can I use PancakeSwap with MetaMask?

Yes for EVM networks. The arXiv paper on EVM compatibility and network effects explains the compatibility layer behind EVM tooling, which is the relevant distinction for MetaMask on BNB Chain, Ethereum, Base, Arbitrum, ZKsync, Linea, and opBNB. For non-EVM networks such as Solana or Aptos, users need a compatible wallet for that ecosystem.

Why is my CAKE on the wrong chain?

CAKE can exist on more than one network. BscScan's BNB Chain CAKE contract is one place to verify the token address, and a balance may not appear if your wallet is connected to a different chain or token contract. Switch to the correct chain and verify the contract address from reputable sources.

Are PancakeSwap swaps custodial?

PancakeSwap swaps are non-custodial when executed from a self-custody wallet. The app and smart contracts route the trade, but the user signs transactions from their own wallet. EIP-20's allowance method reference explains why token approvals still matter.

Where can I check PancakeSwap audits?

PancakeSwap maintains an official audits page listing reports for products such as Infinity, Exchange V3, MasterChef, StableSwap, and other components. Audits reduce unknowns but do not remove all smart-contract, governance, liquidity, or user-signing risk.
